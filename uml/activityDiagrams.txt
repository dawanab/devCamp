# Elements of a UML Activity Diagram:

I. There are 7 different elements: initial state(start point),
activity (action state), action flow, decisions and branching,
guards, final state (end point), and swim lines 
    > initial state is represented by a thick circle with a white
    circle in the center; it's how you represent how a user or 
    system will begin

    > activity (action state) - represented by the boxes on the diagram

    > action flow - represented by a filled-in arrow tip; means that 
    we took a piece of data and took it from one action state to 
    another action state and this action flow is how they're connected

    > decisions (branching) - represented by a diamond square which 
    is called the branching point or decision; represnts a stage in the 
    application where a decision has to be made

    > guards - represented by 'yes' or 'no' options

    > final state - stores the result inside of the system database

    > swim lane - a way to group activities performed by the same actor
    or an activity diagram or to group of activities in a single thread
